Get your printer off vendor firmware.

I have printers from four different brands and I'm tired of each one shipping its own locked-down OS, its own half-broken Klipper fork, and its own services that exist mostly to phone home. Deckhand swaps that for the real thing: stock Klipper or Kalico, Mainsail or Fluidd, your configs backed up. Runs on your laptop. Talks to your printer. That's it.

What it does

Two ways to get there.

Both end the same way: a printer running real Klipper, the web UI you actually like, and a backup you can roll back to if you change your mind.

Path A · Stock keep

Keep the OS, swap the stack

Snapshots what's there, kills the vendor services you don't want, drops in Kalico or Klipper, puts your printer.cfg back. If something goes sideways, the snapshot rolls you back. This is the safe one.

Path B · Fresh flash

Wipe and start clean

Writes a fresh Armbian image to the eMMC and builds the stack from scratch. You lose the vendor mess and any weird config drift. Restore your printer.cfg from a backup at the end if you want.

After install

Stays useful

Snapshots, KIAUH launcher, debug bundles with a redaction preview before you send anything. When you're past needing a GUI for everything, you can just SSH in. Nothing here will fight you.

GUI ↔ shell

Nothing happens that you can't see.

Every wizard screen lists the exact commands it's about to run. Copy them, audit them, paste them somewhere else, or just hit Continue. Your call.

Where the data goes

Nowhere it doesn't have to.

There's no Deckhand server. No login. The app talks to your printer on the LAN and once a run grabs firmware tarballs from GitHub and Armbian. Three hosts total. If you don't trust me on that, the allow-list lives in config/network.yaml and the Doctor screen prints every URL before anything connects.
